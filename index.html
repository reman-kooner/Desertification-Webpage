Impact on Agriculture

Desertification, according to the European Parliament, endangers over 1 billion people in 100 countries by reducing the ability of land to support food production and biological diversity. Desertification is both human-induced and climate-related and can be reversed through proper monitoring and sustainable management. There is no coordinated effort by the EU to combat desertification despite nearly half of its member countries having regions that are affected. Agriculture has a dual function, both degrading the land and providing remedies in the form of sustainable farming techniques.

Human Displacement

Desertification, as defined by the United Nations Convention to Combat Desertification (UNCCD), is one of the biggest current environmental challenges due to unsustainable agriculture, deforestation, and global warming. Dryland degradation endangers biodiversity, food security, and socio-economic stability, and puts 50 million at risk of displacement in the next ten years. The UNCCD, established in 1994, provides international coordination for action to restore land productivity and mitigate the effects of drought, helping 70 countries affected by drought to develop action plans to combat desertification. Health Consequences

Aridification leads to health issues such as increased childhood stunting and reduced water supplies, particularly in vulnerable regions.

  • higher threats of malnutrition from reduced food and water supplies
  • more water- and food-borne diseases that result from poor hygiene and a lack of clean water
  • respiratory diseases caused by atmospheric dust from wind erosion and other air pollutants
  • the spread of infectious diseases as populations migrate.

Global Initiatives

A $500 million Climate Investment Funds program will combat land degradation in Ethiopia with $37 million in CIF funding and the remaining funds provided by co-investors like the World Bank. The program will restore 320,000 hectares of coffee forest, farmland, and rangeland and create a forest registry. It aims to enhance food security, empower small farmers, and integrate climate resilience into national policy.

Sustainable Agriculture

Desertification, the conversion of productive land to dry desert, threatens ecosystems and human health. Regenerative agriculture offers a solution through soil reversal from degradation, encouraging biodiversity, and maximizing water use. Methods like cover cropping, rotational grazing, and decreased tillage avoid erosion, improve soil resilience, and store carbon, reversing climate change while ensuring food production. The investment in regenerative methods can restore degraded lands and prevent desertification, making the world's future more sustainable.
